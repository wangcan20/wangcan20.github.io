About

I am a master student in Biostatistics at Johns Hopkins University. I am currently working with Dr. Mei-Cheng Wang and Dr. Yiqun Chen.

I am broadly interested in developing uncertainty-aware and assumption-lean inference frameworks that integrate statistical principles with ML tools, with a focus on reliability in high-stakes medical applications.

My recent projects span conformal methods for competing-risk survival, autonomous LLM agents for causal estimation, and the MEDAL dataset for evidence-based clinical QA.

Research Interests

  • Distribution-free inference: conformal prediction; robustness under weak assumptions
  • AI for Statistics; Medical AI: autonomous statistical agents; trustworthy medical AI
  • Survival analysis: competing risk models; time-to-event prediction
  • Causal inference: automated causal estimation; evaluation

Projects

Autonomous LLM Agents for Causal Inference

We build an autonomous LLM-based coding agent that generates and refines causal estimators through iterative self-improvement, achieving performance comparable to expert-designed methods.

Two-stage Conformal Predictive Interval for Competing-Risk Survival (Thesis)

We develop a two-stage conformal method for competing-risk survival data, providing model-free support for cause prediction and calibrated intervals under censoring.
